In Liberty City we see life in a Miami ghetto through the eyes of those who live there, as well as those who they almost daily interact with: each other & their friends & families, the police, the county jails, Raiford Prison, the jobs and their influence on the quantity as well as quality of their lives and why money, work and education plays such a large part in their lives. It begins on a street corner where five of the main characters are waiting to be picked up for a construction job. It spirals out of control on their third day at work when the white daughters of the general contractor's make a frivolous play for one of the black laborers, which ends up with them, their fathers and four of the laborers in a courtroom, in which justice becomes secondary to the societal pressures of an all-white jury, in 1968, trying two black laborers, one who is an escaped-felon, on a trumped-up charge that ruins more lives than just those who end up in prison.
