the only true knowledge of our fellow-man is that which enables us to feel with himGeorge Eliots first published work consisted of three short novellas: The Sad Fortunes of the Reverend Amos Barton, Mr Gilfils Love-Story, and Janets Repentance. Their depiction of the lives of ordinary men and women in a provincial Midlands town initiated a new era of nineteenth-century literary realism. The tales concern rural members of the clergy and the gossip and factions that a small town generates around them. Amos Barton only realizes how much he depends upon his wifesselfless love when she dies prematurely; Mr Gilfils devotion to a girl who loves another is only fleetingly rewarded; and Janet Dempster suffers years of domestic abuse before the influence of an Evangelical minister turns her life around.
